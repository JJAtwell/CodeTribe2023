Study Title:

Drowsiness Detection and its contributing factors



Researchers:

Kerrin Klaaste (201727404), Jocelyn Atwell (201800014), Mfundo Monchwe (201726904)


Before agreeing to participate in this research study, we firmly encourage you to read and understand the following explanation of this study. The statement describes the purpose and procedures of the study. Furthermore, it is your right to withdraw from the study at any time. This study has been approved by the Computer Science and Information Technology Department, School of Natural and Applied Sciences at Sol Plaatje University.



Explanation of Procedures

This study is designed to detect drowsiness within computer users along with the factors that contribute towards drowsiness. We are conducting this study to learn more about this question since the majority of the focus in the past was detecting drowsiness within drivers of vehicles. This study shifts focus to those who make use of electronic devices such as computers and laptops. Participation in the study involves completion of a questionnaire that asks you basic questions about your personal preferences with regards to your working environment and daily habits while using your computer or laptop. Thereafter the study will be conducted in a controlled environment along with an environment in which the variables are manipulated. This will take approximately one hour. The interviews will be conducted by one of the researchers, video-taped, photographed and later transcribed for the purpose of data analysis.



Risks and Discomforts

Potential risks or discomforts anticipated from your participation in the study include the spread of COVID-19. We will be taking multiple precautionary measures such as thorough sanitising of all equipment before and after use, the use of face masks as well as minimal face-to-face contact.



Benefits

The anticipated benefit of participation is the research study include the opportunity to aid future computer users in minimising drowsiness while working.



Confidentiality

All of the information obtained during this study will remain confidential during this project. Only the researchers will have access to the study data and information. Personal information and preferences will be obtained via the questionnaires, but this information will only be available for use by the researches. Your names and any other identifying details will never be revealed in any publication of the results of this study, however due to the nature of this study, the user’s Eye Aspect Ratio can only be determined by scanning the user’s face therefore the study will make use of videos and photos of the user. The results of the research will be published in the form of a research paper and may be published in a professional journal or presented at professional meetings. It may also be published in book form. The knowledge obtained from this study will be of great value in guiding professionals to be more effective drowsiness detection and prevention.



Withdrawal without Prejudice

Participation in this study is voluntary; refusal to participate will involve no penalty. You are free to withdraw consent and discontinue participation in this project at any time without prejudice or penalty. You are also free to refuse to answer any question we might ask you.
